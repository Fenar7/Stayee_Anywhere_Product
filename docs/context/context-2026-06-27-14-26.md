## 1. Product Overview & Tech Stack
NextHome is a next-generation SaaS application for managing hostels and co-living spaces. It handles everything from tenant onboarding and bed assignments to rent collection, maintenance requests, and daily meal planning.

- **Framework**: Next.js 16 (App Router)
- **Database**: PostgreSQL (via Prisma ORM)
- **Authentication**: Supabase Auth (Server-side rendering setup with middleware)
- **Styling**: Tailwind CSS v4 + shadcn/ui
- **Architecture Rules**: 
  - Strict Server Components by default; `use client` only where interactivity is needed.
  - Financial Data: All money is strictly stored and handled in **Paise** (integers) to prevent floating-point errors (e.g., â‚ą100.00 is stored as `10000`).

## 2. Roles & Workspaces
The platform is heavily role-based, ensuring strict data isolation:

- **MAIN_ADMIN**: The super-user who owns an entire `Organization`. They can create hostels, view cross-hostel analytics, and manage wardens.
- **WARDEN**: Staff assigned to manage a single specific `Hostel`. They approve tenants, assign beds, manage daily kitchen worklists, and resolve service requests for their assigned building.
- **TENANT**: A resident who has an active `Stay` linked to a `Bed` in a room. They can view rent dues, log service tickets, and submit daily food feedback.

## 3. Completed Workflows & Modules

### A. Foundation & Security
- Fully functioning Supabase auth integrated with Prisma user sync.
- Secure routing via `middleware.ts` which intercepts unauthorized access and correctly routes users to `/admin`, `/warden`, or `/tenant` based on their `UserRole`.
- A generic `RoleShell` and `Sidebar` component mapping the UI based on the user's role.

### B. Tenant Onboarding Lifecycle
1. **New User Registration**: Users sign up and are initially unassigned.
2. **Onboarding Form**: Tenants fill out their profile, emergency contacts, and ID proofs (`/onboard`).
3. **Warden Approval**: Wardens review pending applications in `/warden/onboards`.
4. **Bed Assignment**: Wardens assign approved tenants to specific rooms and beds.
5. **Active Stay**: Once assigned, a `Stay` record is generated, giving the user full `TENANT` access.

### C. Occupancy & Room Management
- **Occupancy Map**: Visual grid showing floors, rooms, and bed statuses (Available, Occupied, Reserved).
- **Bed Management**: Ability for wardens to track exact capacity and utilization.

### D. Financials & Rent Collection
- Automatic generation of rent dues.
- Tenants can upload payment proofs.
- Wardens verify and approve pending payments (`/warden/stays/[id]`).

### E. Service Requests (Maintenance)
- Tenants can log maintenance issues (e.g., "Plumbing", "Electrical") from their dashboard.
- Wardens manage the queue of service requests, assigning them to staff and marking them as resolved (`/warden/service-requests`).

### F. Kitchen & Food Management
- Wardens can publish weekly/daily meal menus (`/warden/food`).
- Tenants view today's menu and submit feedback/ratings for meals (`/tenant/food`).
- Aggregated ratings are shown on the Warden dashboard to adjust kitchen output.

### G. Notifications & Communications
- A centralized notification system (`Notification` table) for sending in-app alerts to tenants (e.g., "Rent due tomorrow", "Service request resolved").
- Real-time inbox for tenants to read and dismiss notifications (`/tenant/notifications`).
